## Core Principles

### 1. Governance Retention (Non-Negotiable)

**Founder Control Structure**:
- **Seed Round**: Founders retain 70%+ voting control through dual-class shares
- **Series A**: Founders retain 51%+ voting control (protective provisions accepted)
- **Long-Term**: Maintain strategic decision-making authority through liquidity events

**Rationale**:
- GTN's transformation methodology requires long-term vision (not quarterly optimization)
- EVOLUTION.LIFE's success came from founder-driven product decisions (not investor committees)
- Network state partnerships require authentic alignment (not profit-maximization pressure)

---

### 2. Traditional SaaS Model (Not Token)

**Why SaaS**:
- ‚úÖ Predictable revenue model (MRR/ARR tracking)
- ‚úÖ Lower customer acquisition friction (credit card vs. token custody)
- ‚úÖ Proven investor playbook (SaaS metrics well-understood)
- ‚úÖ Regulatory clarity (no token securities issues)
- ‚úÖ Faster sales cycles (no token education required)

**Token Optionality Preserved**:
- Year 3+ consideration if network effects proven
- Token launch from position of strength ($3-5M ARR)
- Governance token (not stake-for-access) if launched

---

### 3. Risk-Mitigated Go-to-Market

**Validation Before Capital**:
- Month 1-3: Execute validation sprints (DAO, co-living, individual, enterprise)
- Prove demand with $0 pilots and waitlist signups
- Raise capital ONLY after 3/4 validations succeed

**Phased Revenue Strategy**:
- Phase 1 (Months 1-12): Foundation ($150K ARR)

## Seed Round Structure

### Investment Terms

**Amount**: $2.5M
**Instrument**: Series Seed Preferred Stock
**Valuation**: $7.5M pre-money, $10M post-money
**Dilution**: 25% to investors, 75% retained (60% founders, 15% employee pool)

**Share Structure**:
```
Class A Common (10 votes/share): Founders only
Class B Common (1 vote/share): Employees, advisors
Series Seed Preferred (1 vote/share): Investors

Founder voting power: 60% √ó 10 votes = 85.7% control
Employee voting power: 15% √ó 1 vote = 2.1% control
Investor voting power: 25% √ó 1 vote = 12.2% control
```

**Protective Provisions** (Standard):
- Board seats: 2 founders, 1 investor, 0 independent (investor gets observer rights)
- Approval required for: asset sales >$500K, new equity raises, liquidation preferences changes
- NO approval required for: product decisions, hiring, partnerships, operational matters

---

### Governance Retention Mechanisms

**1. Dual-Class Structure**:
- Class A (10 votes/share) reserved for founders
- Converts to Class B (1 vote/share) upon founder exit
- Maintains founder control through Series A and beyond

**2. Board Composition**:
- Seed: 2 founders, 1 investor observer (no vote)
- Series A: 2 founders, 1 investor, 1 independent (founders retain majority)
- Series B+: 2 founders, 2 investors, 1 independent (founders retain tiebreaker)

**3. Investor Rights Limitations**:
- **NO drag-along rights** (investors cannot force sale)
- **NO participation rights** (no double-dipping on exits)
- **NO liquidation preference >1x** (investors get money back, not 2-3x)
- **NO veto on product roadmap** (founders control product decisions)

**4. Anti-Dilution Protection**:
- Broad-based weighted average (founder-friendly, not full ratchet)
- Protects investors from down-rounds WITHOUT punishing founders

#### Risk 4: Capital Exhaustion Before Series A

**Mitigation**:
- **Runway Monitoring**: Weekly cash tracking, 12-month forward projections
- **Series A Timing**: Begin fundraising at Month 18-20 (not Month 24-27) if burn accelerating
- **Bridge Options**: $500K bridge from existing investors if Series A delayed

- **Monthly PMF Assessment**: Track NPS, retention, expansion revenue
