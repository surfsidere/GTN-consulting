# Classical Copywriting Formulas Reference
## Tactical Execution Frameworks

**Purpose**: Proven copywriting formulas for specific communication goals, each with structure, use cases, and examples.

**Principle**: Choose formula based on goal, not preference. Each formula solves a specific persuasion challenge.

---

## Formula 1: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

### Structure

**P - Problem**: State the issue clearly and concisely
**A - Agitate**: Amplify the emotional impact of the problem
**S - Solution**: Present the brand as the guide who solves it

### When to Use

**Ideal for**:
- Email subject lines
- Social media posts
- Landing page opening sections
- StoryBrand "Problem" section

**Goal**: Create urgency by making the problem feel immediate and painful

### Psychological Mechanism

**Why it works**: Loss aversion‚Äîhumans are more motivated to solve pain than pursue gain. PAS activates this primitive response.

---

### Formula Application

**Step 1: Problem (State It)**

Be specific and concrete. Use customer language.

**Examples**:
- ‚ùå Vague: "Marketing is hard"
- ‚úÖ Specific: "You're spending $2K/month on ads that generate zero qualified leads"

---

**Step 2: Agitate (Make It Hurt)**

Add emotional layers. Connect to internal problem (self-doubt, frustration, fear).

**Techniques**:
- **Quantify the waste**: "That's $24K per year down the drain"
- **Future project**: "At this rate, you'll burn through your runway in 6 months"
- **Identity threat**: "You're starting to question if you're cut out for this"
- **Competitive anxiety**: "Meanwhile, your competitors are growing"

**Examples**:
- ‚ùå Weak agitation: "This isn't ideal"
- ‚úÖ Strong agitation: "Every month without a lead system is another month watching your best competitors pull ahead while you're stuck on the hamster wheel"

---

**Step 3: Solution (Position as Guide)**

Introduce the brand as the helper, not the hero. Focus on transformation, not features.

**Formula**: "[Brand] helps [customer type] [achieve desire] by [unique mechanism]"

**Examples**:
- ‚ùå Feature-focused: "Our platform has CRM integration and automation"
- ‚úÖ Transformation-focused: "Our Simple Lead System helps small business owners generate 15+ qualified leads per week using our proven 3-step framework"

---

### Complete PAS Examples

**Example 1: Email Subject Line**

**Problem**: "Your calendar is empty next week"
**Agitate**: (Implied in recipient's mind: "I might not hit my goals")
**Solution**: "Here's how to fill it with qualified leads"

---

**Example 2: Social Media Post**

**Problem**: "Spent $5K on Facebook ads last quarter. Got 3 leads that didn't convert."

**Agitate**: "That gut-punch feeling when you check your ROI? Yeah, we know it. You're working harder than ever, but your lead pipeline looks like a desert. And that voice in your head is getting louder: 'Maybe I'm just not good at marketing.'"

**Solution**: "Here's the truth: You don't suck at marketing. The system does. We've helped 500+ small business owners escape this exact trap. Want the playbook? Link in bio."

---

**Example 3: Landing Page Opening**

**Problem**:
"Lead generation feels like throwing spaghetti at the wall. Some months you're feast, others you're famine. It's exhausting."

**Agitate**:

---

### PAS Pitfalls to Avoid

‚ùå **Over-agitation**: Don't fear-monger or catastrophize unrealistically
‚ùå **Agitation without empathy**: Feels manipulative; lose trust
‚ùå **Vague problem**: "Things aren't working" is too general
‚ùå **Feature-heavy solution**: List features instead of transformation

‚úÖ **Best Practices**:
- Keep problem specific and relatable
- Agitate with authentic emotional truth
- Solution focuses on customer transformation
- Transition smoothly (don't jump abruptly to "buy now")

## Formula 2: BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

### Structure

**B - Before**: Describe the current frustrated state
**A - After**: Paint the aspirational transformed state
**S - Bridge**: Explain how the brand facilitates the journey

### When to Use

**Ideal for**:
- StoryBrand "Success" section
- Testimonial framing
- Case studies
- Vision-casting sections

**Goal**: Create desire through vivid visualization of transformation

### Psychological Mechanism

**Why it works**: Humans make decisions emotionally, then rationalize logically. BAB triggers emotional desire first, then provides logical bridge.

---

### Formula Application

**Step 1: Before (Current State)**

Paint a vivid, frustrating "day in the life" before transformation.

**Techniques**:
- Use sensory details (what do they see, feel, hear?)
- Include time stamps ("It's 11pm on Sunday...")
- Add internal monologue ("You're thinking: 'There has to be a better way'")
- Be specific (not "busy," but "answering 47 unqualified emails")

**Examples**:
- ‚ùå Generic: "You're stressed about leads"
- ‚úÖ Vivid: "It's Tuesday morning. You open your empty calendar. Again. Three networking events last week. Five hours on LinkedIn. Zero qualified leads. You're starting to wonder if your business model is broken."

---

**Step 2: After (Transformed State)**

Contrast with equally vivid "day in the life" after transformation.

**Techniques**:
- Mirror the "before" structure (same time of day, same frustrations ‚Üí resolved)
- Show external AND internal transformation
- Include unexpected benefits (ripple effects)
- Make it emotionally compelling, not just practically better

**Examples**:
- ‚ùå Flat: "You have more leads"
- ‚úÖ Vivid: "Now it's Tuesday morning. You open your CRM to 14 new qualified leads‚Äîbusiness owners who found your content and raised their hands. You schedule three strategy calls before breakfast. No hustle. No desperation. Just consistent, predictable growth. And that voice questioning your competence? Silent."

---

**Step 3: Bridge (The Mechanism)**

Explain HOW the transformation happens‚Äîthe unique process, system, or methodology.

**Formula**: "Here's how we take you from [before] to [after]: [3-step process]"

**Principles**:
- Keep it simple (3-4 steps maximum)
- Use concrete action verbs
- Show customer agency ("You'll," not "We'll")
- Connect to brand's unique methodology

**Examples**:
- ‚ùå Vague: "We use our proprietary system"
- ‚úÖ Clear: "Here's how we take you from lead drought to consistent pipeline:
  1. We analyze your expertise and ideal client to create a Lead Magnet they can't resist
  2. You attract qualified prospects through our organic content framework (no ads needed)
  3. Our automated nurture sequence turns interested leads into booked calls‚Äîwhile you sleep"

### BAB Pitfalls to Avoid

‚ùå **Unrealistic "after"**: Promising transformation that's not achievable
‚ùå **Weak contrast**: Before and after feel too similar
‚ùå **Complicated bridge**: Process has 10 steps and confuses
‚ùå **Feature-focused bridge**: Explain technical specs instead of customer experience

‚úÖ **Best Practices**:
- Before and after should be emotionally contrasting
- After includes unexpected benefits (ripple effects)
- Bridge is 3-4 simple, customer-centric steps
- Use sensory, specific language (not abstract concepts)

## Formula 3: AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

### Structure

**A - Attention**: Hook with compelling headline
**I - Interest**: Develop curiosity and relevance
**D - Desire**: Build emotional want and aspiration
**A - Action**: Clear, specific call-to-action

### When to Use

**Ideal for**:
- Full sales pages
- Long-form landing pages
- Email sequences (1 email = 1 AIDA component)
- Video sales letters

**Goal**: Guide prospect through complete persuasion journey from cold ‚Üí hot

### Psychological Mechanism

**Why it works**: Mirrors the natural decision-making process‚Äîyou must capture attention before you can persuade. Each stage prepares the next.

---

### Formula Application

**Step 1: Attention (The Hook)**

Goal: Stop the scroll. Make them read the next sentence.

**Techniques**:
- **Shocking stat**: "87% of small businesses fail due to this one marketing mistake"
- **Bold claim**: "What if you could generate leads without spending a dollar on ads?"
- **Direct challenge**: "Still wasting money on marketing that doesn't convert?"
- **Curiosity gap**: "The lead generation secret no one talks about"

**Headline Formula Options**:
- "How to [Desired Outcome] Without [Common Obstacle]"
- "[Number] Reasons Why [Belief] Is Dead Wrong"
- "The Secret to [Desire] That [Authority] Don't Want You to Know"
- "What [Aspiration Identity] Know About [Topic] That You Don't"

**Examples**:
- ‚ùå Boring: "Lead Generation Services for Small Businesses"
- ‚úÖ Attention-Grabbing: "Generate 15+ Qualified Leads Per Week Without Spending a Dollar on Ads"

---

**Step 2: Interest (Develop Relevance)**

Goal: Prove this message is for them. Build curiosity about how it works.

**Techniques**:
- **Qualify the reader**: "If you're a small business owner tired of feast-or-famine lead flow..."
- **Amplify the problem**: "You've tried Facebook ads ($5K burned), cold email (crickets), networking events (time drain)..."
- **Tease the solution**: "What if there was a system that attracted qualified leads organically‚Äîwhile you sleep?"
- **Story hook**: "Three years ago, I was exactly where you are..."

**Structure**:
```
[Attention headline]

‚Üì

If you're [target audience] struggling with [specific problem], this is for you.

You've probably tried [failed solution 1], [failed solution 2], and [failed solution 3].

None of them worked because [why conventional approaches fail].

What if [teaser of unconventional solution]?

That's exactly what I'm about to show you.
```

---

**Step 3: Desire (Build Emotional Want)**

Goal: Make them want the transformation viscerally.

**Techniques**:
- **Success vision (BAB)**: Paint vivid "after" state
- **Social proof**: "Join 500+ business owners who've transformed their lead gen"
- **Authority building**: Credentials, case studies, testimonials
- **Unique mechanism**: "Our proprietary 3-Step Simple Lead System"
- **Contrast**: Before/after, us/them, old way/new way

**Structure**:
```
Imagine [vivid success vision using BAB formula]...

That's the transformation our [unique system name] creates.

Here's how it works: [3-step bridge]

Don't just take my word for it. Here's [Customer Name]:

[Testimonial with specific results]

This is possible for you too.
```

---

**Step 4: Action (Drive the CTA)**

Goal: Remove friction and get commitment.

**Techniques**:
- **Direct CTA**: "Schedule Your Free Strategy Session Now"
- **Risk reversal**: "90-day money-back guarantee‚Äîif you don't see 15+ leads, full refund"
- **Urgency (authentic)**: "We cap onboarding at 10 clients/month. 3 spots left this month."
- **Simplify next step**: "Click the button. Fill out 3 questions. We'll email your custom roadmap."

**CTA Language Principles**:
- Use action verbs: Schedule, Start, Get, Join, Claim
- State clear value: "Free," "Guaranteed," "No Risk"
- Keep friction low: "No credit card required," "Cancel anytime"
- Repeat 3-5 times on page (above fold, mid-page, bottom, sticky button)

**Structure**:
```
Ready to [achieve desire] without [obstacle]?

[Direct CTA Button: "Schedule Your Free Strategy Session"]

Here's what happens next:
1. [Simple step 1]
2. [Simple step 2]
3. [Simple step 3]

üõ°Ô∏è Risk-Free Guarantee: [Specific guarantee]
```

## Formula 4: 4Ps (Promise-Picture-Proof-Push)

### Structure

**P1 - Promise**: Bold benefit statement
**P2 - Picture**: Visualize the transformation
**P3 - Proof**: Evidence of authority/credibility
**P4 - Push**: Compelling call-to-action

### When to Use

**Ideal for**:
- StoryBrand Guide, Success, and CTA sections
- Ad copy (especially Facebook/LinkedIn)
- Video sales letters
- Webinar presentations

**Goal**: Make a bold claim, prove it's achievable, drive action

### Psychological Mechanism

**Why it works**: Combines aspiration (Promise + Picture) with credibility (Proof) and urgency (Push) in rapid sequence. Creates momentum toward decision.

---

### Formula Application

**Step 1: Promise (Bold Benefit)**

Make a specific, measurable claim about the transformation.

**Formula**: "[Customer type] [achieves specific outcome] in [timeframe]"

**Principles**:
- Be bold but believable
- Quantify when possible
- Focus on outcome, not process
- Use customer language

**Examples**:
- ‚ùå Vague: "Improve your marketing"
- ‚úÖ Specific: "Small business owners generate 15+ qualified leads per week in 90 days"

**More Examples**:
- "Busy professionals get fit in 20 minutes per day‚Äîno gym required"
- "First-time investors build a diversified portfolio in 48 hours‚Äîno finance degree needed"
- "Service providers book their calendar full in 60 days‚Äîwithout spending a dollar on ads"

---

**Step 2: Picture (Visualize It)**

Paint a vivid, sensory image of the promise fulfilled.

Use BAB "After" techniques:
- Sensory details (what they see, feel, hear)
- Unexpected benefits (ripple effects)
- Identity shift (who they become)

**Examples**:

**Lead Gen Promise ‚Üí Picture**:

- **Case studies**: Detailed before/after with numbers
- **Credentials**: "Certified by [authority]," "Featured in [publication]"

## Formula Selection Guide

### Quick Decision Matrix

| Goal | Best Formula | Why |
|------|--------------|-----|
| Articulate problem (SB7 Part 2) | **PAS** | Agitation amplifies pain |
| Show transformation (SB7 Part 7) | **BAB** | Before/after contrast creates desire |
| Full sales page | **AIDA** | Complete persuasion journey |
| Short ad copy | **4Ps** | Rapid promise ‚Üí proof ‚Üí action |
| Email subject line | **PAS (condensed)** | Creates curiosity via problem |
| Landing page hero | **4Ps** | Bold promise captures attention |
| Case study/testimonial | **BAB** | Shows real transformation |
| Email nurture sequence | **AIDA (spread across emails)** | Builds trust progressively |

---

### Multi-Formula Combinations

**Sales Page Structure**:
- **Headline**: 4Ps (Promise)
- **Opening Section**: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
- **Mid-Page**: BAB (Success vision)
- **Testimonials**: BAB (Customer before/after stories)
- **Final CTA**: 4Ps (Push with urgency)

**Email Sequence**:
- **Email 1**: PAS (establish problem)
- **Email 2**: 4Ps (promise + proof)
- **Email 3**: BAB (vision of transformation)
- **Email 4**: AIDA (complete journey ‚Üí action)

## Advanced Techniques

### Formula Stacking

Combine formulas within single piece of copy for maximum impact.

**Example: Landing Page Hero Section**

```

```

### Formula Adaptation by Channel

**Social Media (Character Limit)**:
- Use **PAS (condensed)**: Problem in first sentence, agitate in second, solution in third
- Example tweet: "Spending $5K/month on ads with zero ROI? That's $60K/year down the drain. Here's the system 500+ businesses use to generate leads organically: [link]"

**Email Subject Lines**:
- Use **PAS (P only)**: State problem as curiosity hook
- Example: "Your calendar is empty next week" (recipient agitates themselves)

**Video Sales Letters**:
- Use **AIDA (full)**: Each section = 2-3 minute segment
- Structure: Hook (Attention) ‚Üí Story (Interest) ‚Üí Testimonials (Desire) ‚Üí Offer (Action)

## Validation Checklist

After applying any formula, verify:

**Formula Adherence**:
- [ ] All components of chosen formula are present
- [ ] Components flow in correct sequence
- [ ] No skipped steps (common in AIDA)

**Customer-Centricity**:
