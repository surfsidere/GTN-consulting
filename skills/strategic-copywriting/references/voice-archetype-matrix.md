# Voice Archetype Matrix
## Core Values ‚Üí Linguistic Rules Mapping

**Purpose**: Translate abstract core values into concrete linguistic techniques for consistent brand voice.

**Principle**: Voice is not "tone"‚Äîit's the systematic application of language rules that reflect core values.

---

## The Voice Authenticity Problem

**Common Failure Pattern**:
Companies say their values are "innovative" and "customer-focused," then write copy that sounds identical to every competitor.

**Root Cause**: Values remain abstract. No translation into specific writing rules.

**Solution**: Map each core value to concrete linguistic techniques.

---

## Core Value Translation Framework

For each core value, define:

1. **Linguistic Rules**: Specific writing techniques
2. **Sentence Structure**: Length, complexity, rhythm
3. **Word Choice**: Vocabulary tier, jargon level, metaphor types
4. **Formatting**: Visual emphasis techniques
5. **Examples**: Before/after transformations

## Voice Archetypes (12 Primary Types)

Based on Carl Jung's archetypes + brand voice research

### Archetype 1: The Sage (Expert/Educator)

**Core Values**: Knowledge, wisdom, expertise, evidence-based, rigorous

**Linguistic Rules**:
- **Sentence Structure**: Medium-length (15-20 words), declarative statements, logical flow
- **Word Choice**: Precise terminology, data-driven language, academic metaphors
- **Tone Markers**: Confident but not arrogant, authoritative, educational
- **Avoid**: Hype, superlatives, emotional manipulation

**Specific Techniques**:
- Lead with data: "87% of businesses fail because..."
- Use qualifiers: "Research suggests," "Evidence indicates"
- Cite sources: "According to Harvard Business Review..."
- Structure arguments: "First... Second... Finally..."

**Example Transformation**:

‚ùå Generic:
"Our amazing software will transform your business!"

‚úÖ Sage Voice:
"Based on 500+ implementations, businesses using our platform see an average 34% increase in operational efficiency within 90 days. Here's the data."

### Archetype 2: The Jester (Playful/Irreverent)

**Core Values**: Fun, irreverence, authenticity, anti-establishment, joy

**Linguistic Rules**:
- **Sentence Structure**: Varied (short punchy sentences + longer conversational ones), fragments OK
- **Word Choice**: Colloquial, pop culture references, playful metaphors, occasional profanity

## Linguistic Rule Application

### Sentence Structure Patterns

**Sage**: Medium-length, logical flow, transitions
- "First, consider the data. Research indicates that 87% of businesses experience X. Therefore, the optimal approach is Y."

**Jester**: Varied length, fragments, conversational
- "Marketing sucks. We get it. (Everyone does.) That's why we built something different. No BS. Just results."

**Hero**: Short, punchy, powerful
- "Winners don't settle. You built this business to dominate. Step up. Master the game. Own your market."

**Caregiver**: Flowing, inclusive, warm
- "Growing a business is challenging, and you don't have to navigate it alone. We're here to support you, celebrate your wins, and guide you through the tough moments."

---

### Word Choice Guidelines

**Sage Vocabulary**:
- Precise: "indicate," "demonstrate," "evidence"
- Data: "research," "analysis," "findings"
- Educational: "learn," "understand," "discover"

**Jester Vocabulary**:
- Casual: "yeah," "totally," "literally"
- Pop culture: "Game of Thrones," "Netflix binge"
- Playful: "crush it," "nailed it," "boom"

**Hero Vocabulary**:
- Power: "dominate," "master," "conquer"
- Achievement: "win," "excel," "triumph"
- Competition: "best," "elite," "champion"

**Caregiver Vocabulary**:
- Support: "nurture," "guide," "support"
- Growth: "flourish," "thrive," "blossom"
- Community: "together," "family," "belonging"

## Voice Consistency Checklist

Before publishing any copy, verify:

**Archetype Alignment**:
- [ ] Sentence structure matches archetype rules
- [ ] Word choice reflects core values
- [ ] Metaphors align with archetype type
- [ ] Tone markers are consistent

**Value Reflection**:
- [ ] Each core value appears in linguistic choices
- [ ] No contradictions (e.g., "playful" value with formal language)
- [ ] Unique voice (wouldn't work for competitor)

**Clarity**:
- [ ] Message is clear despite voice personality
- [ ] Voice enhances (not obscures) meaning
- [ ] Appropriate for channel and audience

## Multi-Archetype Blending (Advanced)

**When to blend**: If your brand has conflicting values (e.g., "expert" + "approachable")

**How to blend**:
- **Primary archetype** (70%): Dominates sentence structure and overall tone
- **Secondary archetype** (30%): Adds nuance through word choice and specific examples

**Example: Sage (70%) + Jester (30%)**

Pure Sage:
"Research indicates that 87% of businesses experience lead generation challenges. The optimal solution involves systematic process improvement."

Pure Jester:
"Lead gen sucks for basically everyone. (We checked‚Äîit's science. Kinda.) Want the fix?"

Sage + Jester Blend:
"Here's the data: 87% of businesses struggle with lead generation. (Yeah, it's basically everyone.) But research shows a systematic approach works. No BS‚Äîjust proven strategies."
